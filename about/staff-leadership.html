Kristi Gilleland, Whole Person Care Director

Kristi is originally from Gainesville, Georgia and graduated from North Georgia College and State University in 2004 with a B.S. in Psychology. After earning her Master’s degree in Social Work from UGA in 2009, Kristi worked in rural Georgia as a Social Worker for children and families prior to joining the Mercy team in 2010. Kristi is passionate about whole-person care and bringing patient-centered behavioral health to the uninsured in our community. Kristi lives in Athens with her husband, Brad, their son, Asher and dog, Chai. The Gillelands worship at Classic City Community Church.

Bryson Greenwood, Staff Physician

Dr. Bryson Greenwood, a North Carolina Native, started working at Mercy Health Center in 2015. After earning a liberal arts degree from St. John’s College in Annapolis, Maryland, Dr. Greenwood traveled internationally to Budapest, Hungary, where he worked at an international cellular neuroscience laboratory; and to Lima, Peru, where he studied Spanish and met his wife Katie. Along the way, he found his calling in family medicine, where he could combine his interests in science and social justice. He attended medical school and the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill and stayed to complete his residency in Family Medicine.

Laurel Murrow, Medical Director

Dr. Murrow is an Assistant Professor of Medicine at the GRU/UGA Medical Partnership, where she teaches community health to first year students. She received her B.A. from Wellesley College, her M.Sc. from the London School of Hygiene and Tropical Medicine, and her M.D. from Johns Hopkins University School of Medicine. She completed her residency at Emory University and holds Board Certification in Internal Medicine. She serves on the boards of the Athens Health Network, UGA HEROS, and the Georgia Alliance for Health Literacy. She lives in Bishop with her husband, Dr. Jonathan Murrow, and their three daughters: Madeleine, Ella and Sophie. The Murrows are active members of Ashford Memorial Methodist Church, where they teach Sunday school to the lower elementary class.
