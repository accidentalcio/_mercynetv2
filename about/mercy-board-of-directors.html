Robert Finch

Robert graduated from Cedar Shoals High School, Athens and later went on to receive his B.S. degree in criminal justice and an MBA from Albany State University as a Regents award recipient. Robert received his law degree from the Florida Coastal School of Law, where he matriculated as a Governor’s Scholar. Robert is currently President and CEO of The Dean Group,, LLC, a Government Relations consulting firm. Robert is also Associate Pastor, Mount Pleasant Baptist Church, Athens, GA.

Johnny Frantz

Dr. Frantz is a general dentist. He was born and raised in Oconee County right outside of Athens. He graduated from Presbyterian College with a degree in Biology and then the Medical College of Georgia School of Dentistry. Johnny opened his family and cosmetic dental office in Watkinsville in 1994. He and his wife Joanne have two daughters and live in Bishop. They are active members of Classic City Church.

 

Lauren Griffeth

Lauren Ledbetter Griffeth is a loyal Bulldog. With three degrees from the University of Georgia, Lauren finished a PH.D. in Adult Education with a Certificate in Human Resources and Organizational Development in 2013. Lauren held positions in fundraising for higher education and healthcare before she began serving as an Extension Leadership Specialist directing programs for the College of Agricultural and Environmental Sciences. Lauren resides in Watkinsville with her husband Tim and their two children, Tanner and Lillian. They attend Watkinsville First Baptist Church.

Cindy Lester

Cynthia (Cindy) is a graduate of the University of Georgia (ABJ – ‘72) and the Southern Trust School, Birmingham, AL. She has over 30 years of financial services experience, spending 25 of these years in the Personal Trust and Investment Management area. Pryor to joining Synovus Trust Company in 2004, she served as the Private Client Services Executive at Sun Trust Bank – Athens. Cindy currently serves on the Board of Directors of Athens Area Community Foundation, The Cancer Foundation of Northeast Georgia, and Mercy Health Center. She and her husband, Wayne, and married daughters, Dr. Elizabeth Johnson and Dr. Cynthia Shepherd are long time residents of Oconee County. Cindy and Wayne are long time members of Prince Avenue Baptist Church.

Leslie Sinyard

Neil Stevens, Chair

Neil currently serves as President and CEO of Oconee State bank, bringing 28 years of broad based banking experience to the team. Over the years his main focus has been to lead financial institutions to levels of accelerated performance by placing a high emphasis on building a remarkable culture within the organization. He believes that by hiring remarkable people and putting them in a remarkable culture it will yield remarkable results. Neil began his career as a management trainee in 1989 with the Citizens and Southern National Bank. More recently, he spent ten years with KeyWorth Bank as the President and Chief Operating Officer and was a founding member and organizer of KeyWorth. He helped build a remarkable culture at KeyWorth, which grew to approximately $450 million in assets. He helped negotiate a profitable merger with Renasant Bank in March of 2016. Prior to KeyWorth, he spent nine years with IronStone Bank serving as the Area President for the Georgia Market. This included running 21 retail branches, the mortgage operation, private banking, business banking, residential construction and commercial banking.
