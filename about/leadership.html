Members of the Governing Board

Dr. Timothy M. Chester

Timothy graduated with his Ph.D. in Sociology from Texas A&M University in 1999. Since 2012 Timothy has served as the Vice President for Information Technology for the University of Georgia. The vice president for information technology (VPIT), reporting directly to the Provost, is the university’s highest-ranking technology executive. Dr. Chester, his wife Gail, and their dogs Ching and Eddy reside in Athens, Georgia. They worship at Cornerstone Church.

Dan Delamater

Dan DeLamater is the President of Southern Mutual Insurance Company. Dan is a native Atlantan and a graduate of Georgia State University. He married into the Athens community through wife Cory (Genteman). The DeLamaters live in Athens with their daughter Amelia and son Braden. Dan is in the Athens Rotary Club, is a board member of the Athens Touchdown Club, serves on the Clarke County Mentor Program board, and serves on various insurance industry boards. The DeLamaters were married at Emmanuel Episcopal and worship there today.

David Linder

David Linder is a partner with the firm, Fortson Bentley and Griffin in Athens, Georgia, representing individuals and businesses in commercial transactions and corporate matters. Linder received a B.S, M.S., and J.D. from the University of Georgia. He has served on the boards of The Georgia Children’s Chorus and the Food Bank of Northeast Georgia and is an active member of Briarwood Baptist Church.

Alison Norris

Alison is a returning Mercy Health Center Governing Board Member. Alison graduated from the Terry College of Business in 1997. She owns and operates ATN Financial, LLC. Alison is married to Blaine, and they live in Athens with their three children. She is an avid runner and attends Redeemer Presbyterian Church.

Dr. Emory Patterson

Dr. Patterson is board certified by the American Board of Ophthalmology. He is a native of Georgia, raised in Valdosta, and first came to Athens to attend the University of Georgia. He is a practicing physician at Athens Eye Doctors & Surgeons. Dr. Patterson works with Medical Campus Outreach and is involved at Redeemer Presbyterian Church. He and his wife Lauren live in Athens with their three children. Dr. Patterson and his wife Lauren, both volunteer for Mercy Health Center.

Ron Seib

Ron graduated from the Terry College of Business with a Bachelor of Business Administration in 1983. In 1986, he earned his Master of Business Administration (M.B.A.) from Brenau University. He served as the administrator for the Toccoa Clinic for 9 years (1992-2001) and is currently a financial advisor for Edward Jones in Athens. Ron serves on the Athens Rotary Club and he and his wife, Ellen, attend Athens Church.

Neil Stevens, Chair

Neil currently serves as President and CEO of Oconee State bank, bringing 28 years of broad based banking experience to the team. Over the years his main focus has been to lead financial institutions to levels of accelerated performance by placing a high emphasis on building a remarkable culture within the organization. He believes that by hiring remarkable people and putting them in a remarkable culture it will yield remarkable results. Neil began his career as a management trainee in 1989 with the Citizens and Southern National Bank. More recently, he spent ten years with KeyWorth Bank as the President and Chief Operating Officer and was a founding member and organizer of KeyWorth. He helped build a remarkable culture at KeyWorth, which grew to approximately $450 million in assets. He helped negotiate a profitable merger with Renasant Bank in March of 2016. Prior to KeyWorth, he spent nine years with IronStone Bank serving as the Area President for the Georgia Market. This included running 21 retail branches, the mortgage operation, private banking, business banking, residential construction and commercial banking.

Dr. Fred Young

Dr. Fred Young is a physician who has practiced primary care internal medicine in Athens for over 25 years. Currently, Dr. Young is the Athens Regional Health System Vice President responsible for the Athens Regional Physician Group, a network of over 100 primary care and specialty physicians who are affiliated with Athens Regional Medical Center. Dr. Young and his wife, Amy, have five children.
