Dr. Chester, his wife Gail, and their dogs Ching and Eddy reside in Athens, Georgia. They worship at Cornerstone Church.

Bricker Daughtry

Bricker Daughtry maintains a diverse litigation practice with the law firm Fortson, Bentley and Griffin. Bricker represents individuals and corporations in disputes ranging from general business litigation and employment matters to personal injury cases. Bricker earned his law degree from the Cumberland School of Law in 2007. While attending Cumberland School of Law, Bricker served as an Associate Editor of the American Journal of Trial Advocacy. He was also selected by the school’s faculty to participate in the Judge Abraham Caruthers Teaching Fellowship. He received the Scholar of Merit Award for Mediation and Mediator Practice. Prior to attending law school, Bricker earned a B.S. degree in Cellular Biology and a M. Ed. Degree in Science Education from the university of Georgia. He also was a high school teacher and coach at Apalachee High School in Barrow County. Outside of work, Bricker loves spending time with his wife and four children. He also enjoys playing golf, mountain biking, running, baking artisan bread, and exploring Asian cuisines.
