Who is eligible for services at Mercy Health Center?

In order to be eligible for services, patients must be completely uninsured, at or below 150% of the Federal poverty level, and a resident in one of the following counties: Clarke, Barrow, Jackson, Madison, Oconee or Oglethorpe.

How do I schedule an appointment?

In order to schedule a new patient appointment, patients must call 706-425-4044 on Tuesdays from 4pm-5pm. Appointments are scheduled every Tuesday based on availability. Patients are seen by appointment only and no walk-ins are accepted.

Any person interested in scheduling a dental appointment must first become a medical patient by calling the appointment line on Tuesdays. Once you schedule your first medical visit you will be given information on how to schedule a dental appointment. Our dental services are limited to extractions, cleanings and fillings. We do not provide emergency dental care.

What documentation do I need to bring with me?

  • Picture Identification
  • Proof of income, such as paystubs or a notarized letter
  • Proof of residency, such as a letter with a recent date, name and address, or a lease agreement

Do I have to pay anything?

Services are provided free of charge, although every patient is given the opportunity to contribute to their care either through financial donations or volunteering.

I am a current patient. How do I make another appointment?

Mercy’s office hours are Monday-Friday, 8:30am-4:30pm. Clinic hours extend until 8pm T/W/Tr. If you are an existing patient, please call during office hours to schedule an appointment.
